HouseFooL

HouseFooL is a female producer from South London, UK, who has spent 15 years in the industry, starting behind the scenes doing promo and then on to running record labels. HouseFooL was then know as Lisa C, she started the Vinyl Vixens in 2005, a female DJ/Producer collective which was successful in the London underground scene, and on radio stations. Music videos of The Vinyl Vixens can still be seen on YouTube and DJ mixes can be heard on mixcloud. This DJ collective then got picked up by some of London's Best Events Promoters and made resident DJ's at Back to 95, Groove Odyssey and Bobby & Steve presents, along with other brand names, Lisa then became events manager and online admin for these events for quite some time before taking time out to marry her husband, a fellow DJ, and have 2 children, whilst developing her jingles and adverts company, The Advert People, supplying professional adverts/VoiceOvers/jingles/drops to radio, promoters, DJ's, shop chains and more worldwide. Lisa also makes sample packs, which can be downloaded from Beatport. Current releases from HouseFooL can be found on iTunes, Beatport, Traxsource, Juno Download and Spotify, from labels such as Bad Habit Muzik, 18-09 Records and New Politics Records, a label that Lisa took over in the summer of 2014, due to the war in Ukraine where the label was founded. HouseFooL has a publishing/licensing deals with a Chicago based company, with her track "GhosT" and 3 other tracks, and a licensing deal with Broken Records (USA) for the DJ DIVA'S compilation, with her track "Feel Like This", so we should soon hear her music on rotation in Starbucks and on TV/Films real soon. HouseFooL, a mum of 2, is now running New Politics Records full time, and now spends any spare time she can in her studio making her own music and remixing for other artists, as well as doing adverts and jingles for The Advert People. Energy Lab

Having spent the majority of his life in his native Scotland, he moved to Dubai in 2012.


Jamie has always had a love for technology and all things music, and started writing dance music back in 1988 on an Amiga 500. Experimenting with different genres, from Hardcore to Techno, Ambient to House, he eventually settled on Deep House, which is still his favourite House sub-genre to this day.


In 2008, Jamie sent away a demo album to a number of record labels, and got his first EP signed to Alive in Music, a London-based label. The EP featured two Electro House tracks, and was well received by clubbers and DJ's.


Jamie then took a break from music to concentrate on family and work, and ended up being away from music for almost 6 years. In July 2014, he dusted off his trusty studio equipment and started writing again. In less than 3 months, he had a new track signed to New Politics Records as well as 3 remixes for the same label.


When he's not working on music, Jamie likes to relax by taking his dogs for a walk, watching TV or a film, or shopping for clothes in Dubai Mall.
